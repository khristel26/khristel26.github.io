About Me

I am a graduate student in Public Policy at the University of Chicago, with a focus on data analysis and program evaluation. My interests include using data to inform policy decisions, understanding the effects of policy interventions on various populations, and creating data-driven solutions to social problems. Prior to pursuing my Master's degree, I worked for over 4 years in the public sector.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Business Management from Pontificia Universidad Catolica del Peru in 2017. I went on to pursue my Master's degree in Public Policy from The University of Chicago in 2021.

After completing my Bachelor's degree, I worked as a Consultant at Social Security and Health Insurance Program, EsSalud for 1 year and Ministry of Women and Vulnerable Populations for 3 years. I also worked as teaching assistant at the Pontificia Universidad Catolica del Peru for three years. I did my summer graduate intership at Regional Service Center for Africa in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. Currently I'm working as teaching assistant for two courses Cost-Benefit Analysis and Economic Analysis II: Introduction to Cost-Benefit Analysis

In my free time, I enjoy reading books, and traveling to new places.
